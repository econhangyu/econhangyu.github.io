My teaching aims to train students to analyze real-world economic problems under binding behavioral, institutional, and implementation constraints, and to use empirical tools to produce credible evidence for policy design and evaluation. I teach undergraduates, doctoral students, and mid-career public officials from developing countries.

## PhD Supervision

As a faculty member at the [Institute of South–South Cooperation and Development](https://en.isscad.pku.edu.cn/) (ISSCAD), I supervise doctoral students from developing countries who typically return to public service roles after graduation. My advising philosophy mirrors my teaching: I help students identify meaningful questions grounded in contexts they know well, translate those questions into feasible designs, and develop clear written arguments.
