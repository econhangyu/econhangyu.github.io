I am a development economist and Assistant Professor at the [National School of Development](https://en.nsd.pku.edu.cn/) and the [Institute of South–South Cooperation and Development](https://en.isscad.pku.edu.cn/) (ISSCAD) at **Peking University**. My research studies how behavioral constraints shape economic decision-making in poverty and how implementation determines the success of development efforts, using randomized field experiments in Sub-Saharan Africa. Recent work also examines how artificial intelligence is reshaping labor markets and measurement in hiring.

I received my Ph.D. in Economics from the University of Michigan in 2020.
