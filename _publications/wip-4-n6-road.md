description: "We evaluate a major China-financed road renovation in central Mozambique using a household panel surveyed along the road corridor between 2017 and 2025. Exploiting quasi-random variation in construction timing that altered travel times to urban centers, the study identifies the causal effects of improved connectivity. Preliminary findings suggest increased school attendance among more connected households, consistent with enhanced economic opportunities and higher perceived returns to education."
