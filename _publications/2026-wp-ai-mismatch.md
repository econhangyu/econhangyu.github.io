description: "While a growing literature studies AI's effect on labor demand, far less is known about whether AI improves or worsens the match between workers and jobs. Using 1.4 million vacancy–applicant pairs from a major Chinese recruitment platform (2021–2025), we document that overall labor market mismatch has risen — but this trend reverses in occupations more directly exposed to AI after ChatGPT's release in late 2022. Event-study estimates confirm the causal pattern. The mechanism operates through employer-side job redesign: firms in high-exposure occupations posted positions with more tasks, greater skill complexity, and clearer requirement signals, which reduced low-quality applications. Together with the companion AI exposure study, these papers provide a framework for understanding how AI reshapes both the level and efficiency of labor demand — connecting the measurement of new technology to the matching and personnel frictions that are a recurring theme across this research agenda."
