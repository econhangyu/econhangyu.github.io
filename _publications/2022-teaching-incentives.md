description: "A phone-based randomized controlled trial with 2,117 Mozambican adults cross-randomizes tailored teaching (feedback on individual knowledge gaps) with small financial incentives for correct answers on a follow-up COVID-19 knowledge test. The results show strong complementarity: combining teaching with incentives raised test scores by 0.5 standard deviations — 31 percent larger than the sum of the standalone effects — and the gains persisted nine months later. By estimating a theoretically motivated complementarity parameter, the paper clarifies how poor households allocate effort to learning under uncertainty and offers practical guidance for designing cost-effective public information policies."
