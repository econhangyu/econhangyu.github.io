description: "When public health communication uses both teaching and financial incentives, do the two simply add up — or do they interact? In a phone-based RCT with 2,117 Mozambican adults during the COVID-19 pandemic, combining tailored teaching with small financial rewards raised health knowledge by 0.5 standard deviations — 31 percent more than the sum of standalone effects — and gains persisted nine months later. An estimated complementarity parameter shows that teaching raises the return to effort, contrary to the prior expectation of 81 percent of surveyed experts. The study clarifies how households allocate effort to learning under uncertainty and provides practical guidance for designing cost-effective information policies — a question central to the broader research agenda on how behavioral responses, not just access to information, shape demand for health."
