description: "Do teaching and financial incentives substitute or complement each other in promoting learning? In a phone-based RCT with 2,117 Mozambican adults during COVID-19, combining tailored teaching with small financial rewards raised health knowledge by 0.5 standard deviations — 31 percent more than the sum of standalone effects — and gains persisted nine months later. Contrary to 81 percent of surveyed experts, teaching raises the return to effort. The study clarifies how households allocate effort to learning under uncertainty, central to the broader agenda on how behavioral responses shape demand for health."
