description: "Using original household panel data (2016–2021) from Ethiopia's Southern Nations, Nationalities, and Peoples' Region and a doubly robust difference-in-differences approach, we evaluate Ethiopia's Productive Safety Net Program — one of Africa's largest anti-poverty programs. PSNP improves dietary diversity and asset accumulation, but seasonal food shortages remain largely unchanged, partly due to recurrent delays in transfer disbursement. Conducted during civil conflict and administrative restructuring, the study highlights how administrative capacity and delivery timing shape program effectiveness under stress."
