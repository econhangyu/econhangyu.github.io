description: "Do natural disasters generate persistent losses or trigger adaptive reallocation? Combining satellite-based best-track data on Cyclone Idai with longitudinal household surveys, we exploit spatial variation in wind speed for causal identification. More heavily exposed households shifted from agriculture toward non-farm activities — small business, waged employment, and remittance-linked income — with greater use of the financial system. The study extends the broader agenda's focus on behavioral constraints and institutional capacity to settings where large exogenous shocks test household adaptation."
