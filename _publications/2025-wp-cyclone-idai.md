description: "Combining satellite-based best-track data on Cyclone Idai's trajectory with longitudinal household surveys collected before and after the storm, we link predicted maximum wind speed at the community level to a pre-existing household sample, exploiting spatial variation in disaster intensity for causal identification. More heavily exposed households reduced reliance on agriculture and shifted toward non-farm activities — small business, waged employment, and remittance-linked income — with greater interaction with the financial system. The core question is whether such shocks generate persistent losses or trigger adaptive occupational reallocation."
