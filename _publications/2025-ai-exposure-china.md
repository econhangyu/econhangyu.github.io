description: "How is AI reshaping labor demand in China? Using 1.25 million online job postings (2018–2024), we develop a task-based measure of occupational exposure to large language models tailored to the Chinese labor market — a methodological contribution that makes the AI exposure index publicly available for other researchers. After 2022, high-exposure occupations experienced declining market share, rising employer requirements for education and experience, and increased within-occupation inequality — patterns consistent with substitution pressures. These findings contrast with earlier U.S. evidence showing more muted effects, highlighting the importance of institutional context in shaping how new technologies affect labor markets. This line of work extends the research team's broader interest in information frictions, matching, and personnel systems to study how AI transforms labor demand from the employer side."
