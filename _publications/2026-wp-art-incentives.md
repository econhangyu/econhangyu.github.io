description: "In a multi-arm randomized controlled trial with more than 800 newly diagnosed HIV patients in suburban Mozambique, we randomize financial incentives, reminder calls, informational videos, and a stigma-relieving counseling session. Incentives alone raised antiretroviral therapy adherence by 10.6 percentage points. Pairing incentives with reminders — which reduce inattention to daily adherence and imperfect recall of payment eligibility — raised adherence by 24.3 percentage points. We construct an objective adherence measure from refill-timing data and develop a model showing how behavioral frictions dampen incentive responses."
