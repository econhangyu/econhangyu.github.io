description: "In a multi-arm RCT with over 800 newly diagnosed HIV patients in Mozambique, incentives alone raised antiretroviral therapy adherence by 10.6 percentage points, but pairing them with reminders — which reduce inattention and imperfect recall of payment eligibility — raised adherence by 24.3 percentage points. Pharmacy refill-timing data validate a model in which inattention and imperfect memory dampen incentive effectiveness. The results show that demand-side behavioral frictions go beyond static beliefs: dynamic constraints on attention and memory shape everyday health decisions."
