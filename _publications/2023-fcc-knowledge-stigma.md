description: "We evaluate a large PEPFAR-funded program for orphans and vulnerable children in Mozambique using a multi-stage RCT. Contrary to its objective, the program reduced objectively measured HIV testing by 10.9 percentage points (relative to a baseline testing rate of 26 percent) and made treated households more likely to endorse HIV transmission myths and express stigmatizing attitudes. A second experimental stage at endline shows that mini-interventions correcting misinformation or relieving stigma concerns fully offset the program's negative effects — causal evidence that well-resourced programs can backfire when implementation amplifies fear or stigma."
