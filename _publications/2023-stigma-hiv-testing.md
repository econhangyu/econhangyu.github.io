description: "Why does HIV testing remain far below global targets when testing is free? Individuals in Mozambican communities systematically overestimate how much their neighbors stigmatize people living with HIV, even as actual stigma has declined. A three-arm randomized experiment shows that correcting this misperception raised testing by 36 percent — comparable to a cash incentive exceeding half the daily cost of living. By isolating misperceived social norms as a first-order constraint on health investment, the study opens a broader agenda on how behavioral frictions shape demand for health in low-income settings."
