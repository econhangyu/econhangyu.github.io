description: "HIV testing remains below global targets in many high-prevalence settings despite freely available testing. In this solo-authored paper, I show that concerns about social stigma causally suppress HIV testing in central Mozambique. Baseline surveys document that stigmatizing attitudes had declined substantially over time, but individuals systematically overestimated how negatively their neighbors still viewed HIV-positive people. A three-arm experiment separates belief-based and price-based shifts in demand: correcting misperceived stigma raised testing uptake by 36 percent relative to control — an effect comparable to offering a monetary incentive exceeding half the daily cost of living."
