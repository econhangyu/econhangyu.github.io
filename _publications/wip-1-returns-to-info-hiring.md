description: "We study how different recruitment processes affect the performance of community-based health promoters in Mozambique. A randomized design across 59 neighborhoods varies the information available to hiring committees — from minimal qualifications, to interviews, to full information including trial-period performance. Outcomes are measured using objective clinic records on HIV testing and ART refills, as well as non-incentivized health promotion activities. Conceptually, the study treats frontline hiring as a screening problem under information constraints, linking labor selection mechanisms to service delivery outcomes."
