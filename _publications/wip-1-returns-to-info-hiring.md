description: "How should community health workers be selected when hiring committees operate with limited information? This randomized experiment across 59 neighborhoods in Mozambique varies the information available to those committees — from minimal qualifications, to interviews, to full information including trial-period performance — and measures the impact on objective health service outcomes using clinic records on HIV testing and ART refills. By treating frontline hiring as a screening problem under information constraints, the study directly links labor selection mechanisms to service delivery quality. This project extends the supply-side research agenda from diagnosing implementation biases in existing programs to improving the organizational systems — particularly personnel selection and matching — that determine whether programs succeed."
